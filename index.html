GranuMamba: A Multi-Granularity State Space Model for Co-Speech Gesture Generation

Paper Code coming soon! arXiv

Abstract

Generating high-fidelity co-speech gestures requires modeling complex temporal dynamics, ranging from rapid, fine-grained hand movements to slow, global body shifts. Existing approaches typically operate at a single temporal scale, limiting their ability to capture both local precision and global coherence. We propose GranuMamba, a novel framework that explicitly models multi-scale gesture dynamics. It employs two parallel Mamba-based streams to separately encode short-term fine-grained motions and long-term global dynamics. These representations are adaptively fused via a dynamic gating mechanism, enabling context-dependent integration of temporal features. To further encourage specialization, we introduce granularity-aware training objectives, optimizing each stream with a tailored loss for either local detail or global consistency. Experiments on the BEAT2 dataset demonstrate that GranuMamba outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in both objective metrics, including Fréchet Gesture Distance (FGD), and subjective evaluations, generating gestures that are more expressive, coherent, and human-like.

Method

The pipeline of GranuMamba model. Stage 1: A GraphConv-VQ-VAE discretizes gesture motion into a codebook of tokens. Stage 2: Speech, text, and identity features condition two Mamba-based streams: a fine-grained branch for local hand motions and a coarse-grained branch for global dynamics. A gated fusion integrates both, and the GraphConv-Temporal decoder reconstructs realistic gestures.
