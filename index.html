GranuMamba: A Multi-Granularity State Space Model for Co-Speech Gesture Generation

Abstract

Generating high-fidelity co-speech gestures requires modeling complex temporal dynamics, ranging from rapid, fine-grained hand movements to slow, global body shifts. Existing approaches typically operate at a single temporal scale, limiting their ability to capture both local precision and global coherence. We propose GranuMamba, a novel framework that integrates discrete motion priors with multi-scale temporal modeling. First, a GraphConv-VQ-VAE discretizes skeletal motions into a compact codebook of gesture tokens, enhancing semantic richness and structural consistency. Then, two parallel Mamba-based streams are employed to separately encode short-term fine-grained motions and long-term global dynamics. These representations are adaptively fused via a dynamic gating mechanism, enabling context-dependent integration of temporal features. Experiments on the BEAT2 dataset show that GranuMamba outperforms state-of-the-art baselines on both objective metrics and subjective evaluations, producing gestures that are more expressive, coherent, and human-like.

Method

The pipeline of GranuMamba model. Stage 1: A GraphConv-VQ-VAE discretizes gesture motion into a codebook of tokens. Stage 2: Speech, text, and identity features condition two Mamba-based streams: a fine-grained branch for local hand motions and a coarse-grained branch for global dynamics. A gated fusion integrates both, and the GraphConv-Temporal decoder reconstructs realistic gestures.
